About Me

Hey, I'm Louis.

You look great today! And welcome to my website. I'm currently a freshman at Carnegie Mellon University, School of Computer Science. I was born and raised in St. Paul, MN. I grew up spanning the gap between arts and academia as best I could. From grades 1-11, I was a competitive dancer at a local studio, (what?! dance?!) and I took my senior year to pursue more professional performance opportunites in the local arts scene. In school, I loved just about everything thrown at me (but always a little extra with math); and when I built my own gaming PC in 8th grade, I began my descent into the wondrous world that is Computer Science. These days, I'm especially intrigued, excited, and unnerved by AI. As such, I spend much of my free time consuming safety and alignment literature. I've most recently grown fascinated by Mechanistic Interpretability. I can be reached easily at lsloot@andrew.cmu.edu. Anyways, go and enjoy the website; I (my photo) will still be here!

HPE and Dance

This was my first true computer science project. In short, I developed a software to aid my high school's dance teacher. After several ideation meetings and product proposals, we decided on a software that could automate and aid his assessment of students attempting a historically tricky movement. Focusing on OpenCV, Mediapipe, and ChatGPT integration, I delivered a program that can analyze his students' dancing and provide accurate feedback on necessary improvements. (Feb. 2024)

Research

OurCS @CMU (Oct. 2024)

The OurCS research conference is an opportunity for undergrad students to make meaningful research contributions. In conjunction with two undergrad team members and overseen by a PhD in AI Optimization, I trained, tested, and pruned a standard CNN in PyTorch to empirically locate optimal sparsity ratios that can balance performance and efficiency. We presented our findings to a panel of SCS faculty and conference personnel.
